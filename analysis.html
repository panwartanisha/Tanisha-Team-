Key Observations:
  • The immediate lockdown impact was -2.98%, smaller than expected due to offsetting factors
  • Industrial consumption fell sharply (factories, offices closed)
  • Residential consumption increased (work-from-home, increased heating/cooling)
  • Post-lockdown recovery showed +8.39% improvement, but state-wise recovery was highly variable
  • Some states (Punjab +40.18%) recovered quickly, while others (Sikkim -11.85%) continued to decline

Recommendations & Implications

For Energy Planners
  • Regional Diversification: Invest in renewable energy in WR, NR, and SR where consumption is concentrated
  • Resilience Planning: Develop strategies for differential recovery in various regions
  • Peak Management: Address seasonal demand fluctuations with targeted interventions
  • Infrastructure Investment: Focus on high-consuming states for grid upgrades and renewable integration
For Policy Makers
  • Economic Support: Rural areas showed less resilience; targeted support needed for economic diversification
  • Tariff Strategy: Consider differential pricing for industrial vs. residential sectors
  • Sustainability Goals: 45% of consumption from metro cities offers opportunities for efficiency programs
  • Crisis Management: Develop state-specific protocols for handling demand shocks
