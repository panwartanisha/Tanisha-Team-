Exploring Regional Differences: This scene examines how electricity consumption varies across different regions of India due to climate, industrial activity, and population density.

  • Northern Region (NR) - 29.61% of national consumption

Scene 3 COVID-19 Lockdown Impact

The Pandemic Effect: This critical scene reveals the immediate impact of nationwide lockdown (March-June 2020) on electricity consumption across India.

  • Before Lockdown (Jan-Feb 2020): Average 103.10 units per day
  • During Lockdown (Mar-Jun 2020): Average 100.03 units per day
  • Impact Magnitude: -2.98% during lockdown period

Key Insight: While industrial consumption dropped sharply, residential consumption increased, partially offsetting the overall decline during lockdown.

Lockdown Periods

Full Lockdown:
Mar 25 - May 30, 2020

Partial Lockdown:
Jun 1 - Jun 30, 2020

Data Records:

Scene 4 Recovery Analysis

The Path to Recovery: Following the lockdown, this scene examines how different states rebounded at varying rates based on local economic activity resumption.

  • Post-Lockdown (Jul-Dec 2020): Average 108.43 units per day
  • Recovery Rate: +8.39% improvement from lockdown levels
  • State Variation: Recovery rates ranged from -12% to +40% across states

Key Insight: Recovery was not uniform - states with diverse economies (agriculture + industry) recovered faster than those dependent on a single sector.

Recovery Leaders

Highest Recovery:
Punjab (+40.18%)

Strong Recovery:

Slowest Recovery:

Scene 5 Key Findings & Conclusions

Summary of Insights: This final scene brings together all the insights to paint a comprehensive picture of India's electricity consumption during this critical period.

  • COVID-19 lockdown caused significant but not catastrophic impact on electricity consumption
  • Regional disparities in consumption reflect economic structure and development levels
  • Recovery patterns show economic resilience but at differential rates across states
  • Metropolitan states recovered faster due to diverse economic bases
  • Agricultural states showed more stable demand throughout the period

Takeaway: Understanding regional electricity consumption patterns is crucial for policy-making, infrastructure planning, and economic resilience strategies.
